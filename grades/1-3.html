Year Map

Grades 1-3 planning sequence

Use this as the main pacing page for younger learners.

Quarter 1

Build classroom language and oral confidence

Focus on naming, routines, short responses, and early sound awareness.

  • WIDA focus: Entering
  • Themes: school, self, family, classroom routines
  • Priorities: oral language, phonological awareness, simple frames

Quarter 2

Grow early literacy and descriptive language

Students begin combining words, pictures, and patterned sentences.

  • WIDA focus: Entering to Emerging
  • Themes: weather, clothes, food, helpers
  • Priorities: decoding, shared reading, oral retell

Quarter 3

Move into retelling and explanation

Students retell short texts and describe processes with support.

  • WIDA focus: Emerging
  • Themes: animals, habitats, life cycles
  • Priorities: sequencing, vocabulary, guided writing

Quarter 4

Strengthen independence and discussion

Students expand toward comparing, explaining, and partner talk.

  • WIDA focus: Emerging, with some Developing
  • Themes: plants, celebrations, simple inquiry projects
  • Priorities: compare/contrast, sentence expansion, reading stamina

Priorities

What to emphasize

Core teaching priorities

  • Oral language and classroom participation routines
  • Phonological awareness, phonics, and high-frequency language
  • Patterned sentence building and shared writing

Assessment moves

  • Use short oral checks and picture-supported listening tasks.
  • Collect sentence samples over time.
  • Regroup often based on phonics and oral language evidence.
