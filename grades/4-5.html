Planning focus

  • Keep content connected to homeroom themes whenever possible.
  • Differentiate the language demand, not just the amount of work.
  • Move students from naming and describing toward comparing and justifying.

Year Map

Grades 4-5 planning sequence

Use this as the main pacing page for upper elementary EAL.

Quarter 1

Build survival English and core sentence patterns

Focus on identifying, describing, and informing.

  • WIDA focus: Entering, with some students moving into Emerging
  • Themes: classroom community, routines, family
  • Grammar: nouns, pronouns, articles, adjectives, prepositions

Quarter 2

Move into explanation and comparison

Students begin stretching beyond naming into short explanations.

  • WIDA focus: Entering to Emerging
  • Themes: communities, weather, food, habitats
  • Grammar: possessives, demonstratives, conjunctions, simple future

Quarter 3

Expand academic language

Students work more consistently with explanation, comparison, and early justification.

  • WIDA focus: Emerging
  • Themes: science, geography, biographies
  • Grammar: modals, irregular verbs, comparatives, adverbs of frequency

Quarter 4

Strengthen independence and justification

Students consolidate emerging proficiency and prepare for more independent work.

  • WIDA focus: strong Emerging, with some Developing
  • Themes: environment, inventions, world cultures
  • Grammar: subordinating conjunctions and comparative structures

Month-by-month view

Month Unit Language focus Grammar focus

4 Food, health, and habits Explain, inform Object pronouns, simple future
5 Animals and habitats Compare, explain Irregular nouns, comparative adjectives
6 Maps, places, and communities Describe, compare, inform Compound prepositions and conjunctions
7 Science processes and experiments Sequence, explain, justify Auxiliary verbs, modals, adverbs of frequency
8 Biographies and important people Summarize, explain, analyze Irregular verbs and sequencing language
9 Environment and human impact Compare, evaluate, justify Subordinating conjunctions, comparatives, superlatives
10 Review and project month Summarize, present, justify Spiral review based on student need

Priorities

What to emphasize

Core teaching priorities

  • Language through content, not isolated drills
  • Sentence expansion, explanation, and comparison
  • Supported reading and writing tied to current units

Assessment moves

  • Use weekly oral checks and short written samples.
  • End units with simple speaking or writing performance tasks.
  • Regroup when students show changes in language demand they can handle.
