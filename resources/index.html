Resource Guide

Useful tools, grouped by classroom purpose

These blurbs keep the page practical. Teachers can quickly see what each tool is for and decide whether it supports reading, writing, oral language, or classroom communication.

R

Reading

Raz-Kids

Leveled digital books and listening-reading support for guided reading, independent reading, and home practice.

Open Site
B

Content Support

BrainPOP ELL

Short, structured language lessons that help with grammar, vocabulary, and listening in a clear sequence.

Open Site
E

Reading Library

Epic

High-interest digital texts for reading volume, topic support, and independent browsing at different levels.

Open Site
RW

Comprehension

ReadWorks

Passages and question sets for building comprehension, background knowledge, and supported academic reading.

Open Site
L

Foundations

Lalilo

Early literacy and phonics practice that can support younger learners and students needing foundational reinforcement.

Open Site
S

Classroom Platform

Seesaw

Useful for recording oral language, collecting student work, and sharing simple multilingual tasks with families.

Open Site
Q

Vocabulary

Quizlet

Helpful for vocabulary review, picture-word matching, and repeated practice with targeted unit language.

Open Site
P

Collaboration

Padlet

Good for shared word banks, quick responses, picture prompts, and low-pressure class participation.

Open Site
W

Practice

Wordwall

Fast game-based practice for vocabulary, categories, sentence patterns, and quick revision activities.

Open Site
M

Planning and Support

Magic School

Can help generate scaffolds, sentence frames, quick differentiation ideas, and teacher planning support.

Open Site
D

Language Support

Duolingo for Schools

Best used as extra practice and confidence building, not as the main instructional tool for school EAL goals.
