WIDA Developing

Learners using stronger academic language with growing independence.

Quick Guide

What to expect and what to provide

These students can handle more sustained language tasks and should be pushed toward precision, evidence, and independence.

Students can often

  • summarize and explain using connected language
  • compare ideas, support claims, and discuss with peers
  • read and write with less sentence-level scaffolding
  • sequence events with temporal language and support opinions with examples

Teachers should provide

  • more open-ended discussion and writing tasks
  • academic vocabulary, model texts, and feedback on precision
  • opportunities to justify, analyze, and reflect
  • small-group discussion tasks that ask learners to build on others' ideas
